Geographic Information Systems (GIS) - Part A

A working knowledge of Geographic Information Systems (GIS) and related cartographic strategies is increasingly essential for addressing complex, “glocal” issues such as measuring the intensity of a heatwave, improving pandemic preparedness, locating supply chain disruptions, or accelerating humanitarian relief in data-scarce locations.

Thematic Mapping & Cartographic Design

This selection of thematic maps were made with QGIS to showcase different types of use cases and applications. The cartographic, data management and spatial analysis skills that are featured are also used in the fields of international development, urban planning and environmental management skills.

GIS components & methodologies include:

  • Cartographic conventions for effective public dissemination
  • Adding further contextual information to a given map through inset maps, scale bars, labels, orientation, etc.
  • Multiscale mapping methodologies through map projections/reprojections & zonal statistics
  • Choropleth maps by using aerial units and population normalization (i.e. mapping census data
  • Geoprocessing & spatial analysis (i.e. measuring key features: overlay, proximity, buffer)
  • Mapping vulnerability-exposure, vulnerability, and risk modeling for decision support
  • Crisis mapping, participatory GIS, crowdsourcing and Volunteered Geographic Information (VGI)
